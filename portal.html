Mentorship Portal

Website Application that connects students new to tech with experienced mentors

Client

Tech+ UW

Service

Product Management

Tools Used

Linear, Figma, Miro

Completion Date

Ongoing

Overview

Background

Tech+ is a student organization at the University of Waterloo that aims to support students, from all backgrounds, to be confident, knowledgable, and empowered to pursue a career in the tech industry. One of their key initiatives is the Mentorship Program, which connects over 500 students with experienced upper-year mentors. These mentors provide advice, insights, and resources to help students navigate their careers in tech. To help facilitate these connections, a microsite has been built on the Tech+ website, called the Portal. Here, students can search for mentors by their area of expertise, program and term.

My Role

The Mentorship Portal needed a redesign to make it more user-friendly and to consolidate all mentoring activities into a single platform. My responsibilities included conducting user interviews to understand their needs, defining the features for a new version, writing product requirement documents (PRDs), and leading the design team in developing a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) for the redesigned portal.

Discovery

To better understand how users engage with our Mentorship Portal, I interviewed six pairs of mentors and mentees who had used Version 1 of the Portal. These interviews focused on university students, our primary audience. I wanted to know what they thought about the current microsite, which only allowed them to connect with mentors. By talking to them, I aimed to figure out what features they would want in an all-in-one mentoring platform and any problems they faced with the current system. This information will help us improve the Portal to better meet the needs of our users.

Insights & Issues

  • Mentees would like to browse for general chats and look for people with specific knowledge
  • There is friction in starting a conversation with mentors (that they aren't paired with).
  • Functionality to categorize mentors by areas of interest
  • Some people found it hard to stay organized (keep track of documents, upcoming events, times and places) through emails as it quickly gets cluttered
  • Difficulty in finding suitable upper years to talk to outside of the mentorship program

Exploration

While exploring product strategy, I created an empathy map to extract patterns of user needs and frustrations. Using the research insights as feature value propositions, I also created a list of features to determine must-haves for an MVP website. Prioritization was determined with an impact vs effort analysis.
